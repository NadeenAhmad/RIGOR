The table is part of a medical laboratory database and the ontology is based on the FMA (Foundational Model of Anatomy) and the Ontology for Biomedical Investigations (OBI).

Here's an an example of what the output should look like:


<http://example.org/labour/1> a <http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/FMA_12345> ;
    <http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000001> "2019-03-20"^^<http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema#date> ;
    <http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000070> 1.2 ;
    <http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000071> 1.5 ;
   ...
    <http://purl.obolibrary.org/obi/OBI_0000065> "some value".

The ontology mapping is as follows:

- id: http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/FMA_12345
- patient_id: http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000001
- created_at: http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000002
- date_laboratory_analysis: http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000003
- ASAT_mol_per_l: http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000004
- ALAT_mol_per_l: http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000005
- AP_mol_per_l: http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000006
- GGT_mol_per_l: http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000007
- Lipase_mol_per_l: http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/OBI_0000008
